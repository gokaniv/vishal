Book 1

Evaluate the integral \[\int \sin^2 x dx \,. \] Recall \(\cos 2x = \cos^2 x - \sin^2 x\). Also note that \(\sin^2 x + \cos^2 x= 1\). Thus \(\cos 2x = 1- 2 \sin^2 x\). Solving for \(\sin^2 x\) gives \[\sin^2 x = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2}\cos 2x.\] Thus substituting this relation into the desired integral gives \begin{align*} \int \sin^2 x dx &= \int \bigg[\frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2}\cos 2x \bigg] dx\\ &= \int \frac{1}{2} dx - \frac{1}{2}\int\cos 2x \, dx\\ &= \frac{x}{2} - \frac{1}{4} \sin 2x + C\,. \end{align*} This result was confirmed using Mathematica.
